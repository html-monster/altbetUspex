How it works

Trading Basics:

Alt.bet allows you to make predictions on future events either by buying or selling contracts in the probable outcome. Each outcome has a probability between 1 and 99 percent. We convert those probabilities into monetary values in real accounts or virtual currency in free accounts.

For example, Bettor A thinks in sporting event has at least a 75 percent chance of Team X to win so he or she offers 75 cents for a unit. alt.bet matches Bettor A offer with that of Trader B, who is willing to pay 25 cents for Team Y unit. Each trader now owns number of units in the market for this event on opposite sides.

The prices of units will change over time as probabilities change and both bettors could decide to buy or sell their units at any time. A key to success alt.bet is knowing when to buy and sell in order to take a profit or prevent a loss.

If Team X wins, its units are redeemed at $1. Team Y’s units become worthless. If Team X does not win before the market closes, bettors holding units in Team Y will be paid $1, while Team X’s units will be worthless.

Choosing Markets

Every market has a specification about a potential future event. Some markets feature events that have simple outcomes based on these specifications, while others offer multiple possible outcomes. Each of these options is known as a ‘contracts’.

Besides main board, every market has its own page showing the latest prices and trading metrics available. Pay particularly close attention to the Rules and Specifications for each market. These describe the criteria that will be used to decide whether your prediction comes true or not.

Placing An Order (Bet)

Placing an order or bet at Alt.bet no different than at a regular sportsbook. Simply click on the box showing the best price available, enter your desired number of units, and press BUY/SELL. The unit amount in the box shows the current depth of liquidity available to bet at that price.

Buy (Back) and Sell (Lay)

To ‘Buy’ a selection in a market is to place a bet for something to happen. This is like a bet that you would place with a regular sportsbook and is explained above.

To ‘Sell’ a selection in a market is to place a bet for something NOT to happen, or in other words, bet against something happening.

Example:

If you want to Sell Team Y, or back them NOT to win, the best price available is $.36. Click on the red box and your order will appear in the order pad, as below. Enter your size and press “Submit”.

If you have bet confirmation selected, confirm your bet by clicking on the Place Order button.

Market Exposure

One of the basic principles of Alt.bet is that a customer is only allowed to risk funds that are available in their account.

Market exposure is the term that represents the amount of money that has been reserved to cover the unmatched and matched orders placed on your account.

Each time you place an order (a bet), or make a change to an unmatched order, your market exposure will be adjusted accordingly.

Alt.bet will also reserve funds to cover the trading fees you will have to pay once your order has been settled.

Liquidity Providers and Market Makers

In order to provide deep liquidity and ensure transparent and continuously flowing market, Alt.bet will facilitate its business not only to retail bettors but also to Liquidity Providers (LPs) and licensed gaming operators – Market Makers ( MMs).

Market Makers (MMs) are trading members that act as market makers by committing to provide liquidity in an instrument listed on Alt.bet.

Market Makers (MMs)enhance alt.bet markets because they protect against variations in volatility on the market, guarantee transactions at all times at the best price and support the volume of transactions in the Central Order Book.

Liquidity Providers (LPs) – are trading members who act on agency basis by routing customers’ orders to the exchange.
